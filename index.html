About Me

"I transform complex physical phenomena into functional sensing devices — from concept to publication."

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Optoelectronics Laboratory of Politecnico di Bari, Italy, leading the simulation-driven design of PIC-based photonic sensors for space applications within the EU-funded NODES and InPhoMiR projects. Currently based in Bari, Italy — available for new positions from October 2026.

My work sits at a rare intersection: deep photonic sensor design — spanning GMR structures, microring resonators, Bragg gratings, radiation detectors, and integrated optical gyroscopes — combined with expert-level multiphysics simulation across electromagnetic, thermal, mechanical, and radiation domains. My COMSOL expertise, built over a decade of consulting and industrial training, is among the deepest in the field.

I hold a PhD in Photonics and carry genuine hands-on experience from an ISO Class 7 cleanroom — wire bonding, die bonding, packaging of SLDs, diode lasers, and IR detectors. I understand not just the simulation, but the engineering reality behind it.

Over 12 years of mentoring graduate researchers and teaching at multiple universities have sharpened my ability to communicate complex physics clearly and build capable teams. I am not a generalist — I am a specialist who knows exactly where depth creates value.
